Section 1
How UP Makes Money
Revenue Stream 1: Project-Based Pricing

This is the core revenue engine. One price. Everything included. No per-seat nonsense.

For the General Contractor
$15,000/year
Includes 3 projects. Every additional project: $5,000.
Estimating, bidding, project management, compliance, field ops, document management, financial management. Everything.

The GC does not absorb this cost alone. Here's how the project fee gets spread across the team:

Cost Distribution — How a $5,000 Project Fee Works
GC wins a $2M hospital renovation
25 subcontractors on the project
Project fee: $5,000
$5,000 ÷ 25 subs = $200 each
When does the sub pay? On their first payment through UP.
Sub gets first pay app approved for $80,000. UP deducts $200 automatically. Sub receives $79,800.
Done. One time. Never again on that project.
GC pays $0 for this project (absorbed by subs)
Or GC splits it — GC pays $2,500, subs split $2,500. Configurable per project.
For the Subcontractor

The sub doesn't "buy" UP. They get invited to a project.

  • One-time project fee: $100–$200, deducted from first payment
  • Full access to UP for that project — scheduling, documents, pay apps, compliance, messaging
  • Want UP for your own business? $200/month individual seat — manage your crews, estimating, and books
For the Individual
$200/month
One person. Full UP access.
Estimating, document analysis, cost lookups, compliance checks, AI assistant.

This is the product-led growth entry point. One estimator tries it → shows their PM → PM shows the owner → company converts to project-based pricing at $15K/year.

Revenue Stream 2: Embedded Finance

This is the long-tail revenue multiplier. Not day 1. This is the Toast model: Toast earns 6x more from payments than from software subscriptions.

  • When UP processes payments (ACH, cards), UP earns 48–58 basis points
  • A $30M GC flowing payments through UP = ~$150,000/year in transaction revenue alone
  • Day 1: GCs pay however they want. UP doesn't force it.
  • Month 6+: UP offers faster processing, earned wage access, materials financing
  • Year 2+: Most payments flow through UP because it's easier. Transaction revenue scales.

Revenue Stream 3: Stripe Metered Usage
  • Every project gets an AI usage allocation included in the $5K project fee
  • Heavy users who exceed the allocation pay overage via Stripe metered billing
  • Same model as Claude — generous allocation, only power users hit overage
  • Estimated 10–15% of projects hit overage. Average overage: $500–$2,000/project

How UP Makes Money

1. $15K/year (GC) or $200/mo (individual)
2. $5K/project spread across all subs
3. Overage on heavy AI usage (Stripe metered)
4. Transaction fees on payments (long-tail)
Target: 80%+ gross margins on all layers

Bottom of Funnel: Network Effect (Viral)

This is the WhatsApp model. Users pull other users in.

  • GC starts a project on UP → invites 25 subs
  • Each sub now has UP on their phone
  • Sub works with 5–10 other GCs → tells them about UP
  • K-factor: 2.5–4.0 — each customer brings 2.5–4 new customers
  • At K > 1.0, growth is self-sustaining without marketing spend
