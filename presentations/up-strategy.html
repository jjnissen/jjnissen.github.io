Project Delivery Methods

How a project is delivered determines who controls what, when decisions are made, and which software tools add value. Each method creates different data flows.

Design-Bid-Build (Hard Bid)

Declining

19% of spending

Plain English: Owner hires an architect to design everything first. Then contractors bid on the finished plans. Lowest price wins. The contractor has zero input during design.

Software value: Better estimating tools to win bids with tighter margins. Takeoff automation. Bid comparison.

Design-Build

Growing

44% of spending

Plain English: One company handles both design and construction. The owner has a single point of contact. Design and building happen simultaneously, overlapping.

Software value: Integrated document, cost, and schedule management. Real-time collaboration between designers and builders. Change tracking.

CMAR (Construction Manager at Risk)

Growing

37% of spending

Plain English: The contractor joins during design as an advisor. They guarantee a maximum price (GMP) and are financially responsible if they exceed it. Scope evolves as design progresses.

Software value: Continuous live cost tracking as scope changes. Change management. Budget-to-actual comparison updated in real time.

IPD (Integrated Project Delivery)

Emerging

<5% of spending

Plain English: All parties — owner, architect, contractor — sign one contract. They share profits and losses. If the project comes in under budget, everyone benefits. Over budget, everyone shares the pain.

Software value: Transparent financial tracking visible to all parties. Shared risk/reward dashboards. Full audit trails.

Section 4: Business Model
Float 4: AiM + VLM

Document intelligence and vision language models purpose-built for construction. VLM (Vision Language Model): AI that understands both images and text — can look at a construction drawing and describe what it sees. AiM: UP's proprietary document intelligence layer.

Capabilities

Drawing Classification
Automatically identifies drawing type (floor plan, elevation, section, detail, MEP, structural) from uploaded PDFs.
Text Extraction from Plans
Reads dimensions, room names, material callouts, and specifications directly from construction drawings. OCR + semantic understanding.
Photo Classification
Categorizes jobsite photos by trade, location, and progress stage. Feeds into daily reports automatically.
BIM/IFC Ingestion
Reads 3D building models (BIM = Building Information Modeling; IFC = Industry Foundation Classes, a standard file format) and extracts quantities and specifications.
Automated Takeoff
Measures quantities from drawings (linear feet of pipe, square footage of drywall, etc.) to support estimating. Takeoff = measuring quantities from plans for cost estimating.

Revenue Model

Per-Document Processing$0.50 – $5.00
Premium AI FeaturesIncluded in higher tiers
Metered Usage (via Stripe)Overage billing
Base IncludedStandard processing in subscription

Competitive Advantage

Generic AI cannot classify a mechanical riser from an electrical panel. Construction documents have domain-specific conventions (symbol libraries, abbreviations, layering standards) that require specialized training data. Every document processed on UP improves the model for all users.
